  • Algebra I

We will review this theory as we go along, so don’t sweat it if you’re not an expert or haven’t learned the material yet.

Syllabus

In this mini-course, you will learn about summations. Summations are a topic that appears on the AMCs frequently. Elementary closed forms are necessary throughout the test. More advanced techniques will usually only show up in the later questions. Summation techniques often prove convenient for the AIME. We can often use them to solve demanding questions in algebra, combinatorics, and number theory. Summations are particularly common in contests such as HMMT, CMIMC, and Purple Comet.

This mini-course will cover four main topics about summations, which are:

  1. Introduction and Closed Forms

    In this class, you will learn summation notation, as well as closed forms for several ubiquitous sums, such as arithmetic series, geometric series, and arithmetico-geometric series. You will learn how to apply these tactics to rudimentary summation problems on the AMC 10 and other high school competitions.

  2. Manipulations

    In this class, you will learn elementary summation techniques found throughout the AMC tests. These include strategically grouping terms of a summation, adding and multiplying summations together, in addition to using induction. You will explore how we can often interpret base representations and repeating fractions as summations in disguise. We will derive formulas for the sum of squares, cubes, and other more complicated summands.

  3. Splitting the Summand

    A particularly ubiquitous tool in summation problems is the idea of splitting the summand into multiple parts. You will learn how to simplify a complicated sum into two or smaller sums that are more easily calculable. You will also learn how to make a sequence telescope through the method of partial fractions.

  4. Advanced Strategies

    In this class, we introduce some advanced strategies, such as swapping the order of summation. You will learn that summations do not apply to only Algebra problems. They also apply to exercises in combinatorics and number theory. Finally, we will tackle the most difficult questions on the AMC 10/12, AIME, and beyond using the material in this course.
