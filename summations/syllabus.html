Syllabus

In this mini-course, you will learn about summations. Summations are a topic that appears on the AMCs frequently. Elementary closed forms will be needed throughout the test. More advanced techniques will usually only show up in the later questions. Summation techniques often prove most useful on the AIME and can often be used to solve difficult questions in algebra, combinatorics, and number theory. Summations are particularly common in contests such as HMMT, CMIMC, and Purple Comet.

This minicourse will cover four main topics about summations, which are:

  1. Introduction and Closed Forms

    In this class, you will learn summation notation, as well as closed forms for several ubiquitous sums, such as arithmetic series, geometric series, and arithmetico-geometric series. You will learn how to apply these tactics to basic summation problems on the AMC 10 and other high school competitions.

  2. Manipulations

    In this class, you will learn elementary summation techniques that are found throughout the AMC tests. These include strategically grouping terms of a summation together, adding and multiplying summations together, as well as using induction. You will explore how base representations and repeating fractions can often be interpreted as summations in disguise. We will derive formulas for the sum of squares, cubes, and other more complicated summands.

  3. Splitting the Summand

    A particularly ubiquitous tool in summation problems is the idea of splitting the summand into multiple parts. You will learn how to simplify a complicated sum into two or more smaller sums that can be more easily dealt with. You will also learn how to make a sequence telescope through the method of partial fractions.

  4. Advanced Strategies

    In this class, you will be introduced to some advanced strategies in summations, such as swapping the order of summation. You will learn how summations are found not just in algebra, but in combinatorics and number theory problems. Finally, we will tackle the hardest questions on the AMC 10/12, AIME, and beyond by using the material in this course.
