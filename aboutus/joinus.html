Join Us

ALP is always looking for more staff members to expand our resources and develop our community. Please apply here if you're interested in becoming a staff member at ALP. The descriptions of each position, along with other information, are provided below.

Front End Developer

Collaborate with other team members to develop and update our website. We expect the following: communicating with others on any issues, creating and updating timelines, and re-inforcing deadlines during the development process. Web-development experience with languages such as HTML, CSS and JavaScript are required. We also recommend knowledge of and or experience with React.js, Node.js, and or JQuery. The time commitment is roughly 3 to 5 hours per week but is very flexible.

Instructor/TA

(IF you choose to apply for this position, you MUST also apply for the Content Creation position.) Alongside working as a content creator, creating resources such as handouts, problem sets, etc., in this position, you will also take on the role of being an instructor or teaching assistant. You will facilitate online discussions between instructors and students. You will also help teach and or assist in some of our live lectures. The requirements and workload are the same as our content creation position for the most part (as detailed below). HOWEVER, we do recommend previous teaching experience, and during our lecture seasons, the time commitment is roughly 7 to 14 hours per week and is not flexible for that matter.

Content Creator

As a content creator, you help create ALP's material and resources. These include but are not limited to: the corresponding handouts for respective lectures (including the problem set), assist in managing projects like mini-courses or other ideas, etc. We are mainly looking for people comfortable creating content based on higher levels of competition math than our current AMC-oriented resources. (This is because we are interested in expanding onto a bigger platform and satisfy a more broad spectrum of needs from the math community.) We highly prefer applicants who have at least an AIME and or MathCounts Nationals qualification. The time commitment is flexible, roughly at 5-7 hours per week.

Discord Moderator

This position is very self-explanatory; essentially, you moderate our Community Discord Server. This position includes warning and muting people (with reason), banning people if necessary, etc. There are no requirements. However, we prefer you have previous moderation experience on relevant platforms and know-how bots like Carl-bot or Altdentifier function/work. Time commitment varies on a day-to-day basis.
