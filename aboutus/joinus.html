Join Us

ALP is always looking for more staff members to expand our resources and develop our community. Please apply here if you're interested in becoming a staff member at ALP. The descriptions of each position, along with other information, are provided below.

Front End Developer

Collaborate with other team members to develop and update our website. We expect the following: communicating with others on any issues, creating and updating timelines, and re-inforcing deadlines during the development process.

We require expertise in languages HTML, CSS, and JS. We also recommend proficiency in JavaScript Frameworks and Bootstrap/Bulma. The time commitment is roughly 3 to 5 hours per week but is very flexible.

Content Creator

As a content creator, you help create ALP's material and resources. These include but are not limited to: the corresponding handouts for respective lectures (including the problem set), assist in managing projects like mini-courses or other ideas, etc. We are mainly looking for people comfortable creating content based on higher levels of competition math than our current AMC-oriented resources. (This is because we are interested in expanding onto a bigger platform and satisfy a more broad spectrum of needs from the math community.) Therefore, we highly prefer applicants who have at least an AIME and or MathCounts Nationals qualification. (An Olympiad qualification is all the better.) The time commitment is flexible, roughly at 5 to 7 hours per week.

Also, you might become a lead instructor or teaching assistant (TA) along with this position if you express interest. If so, you will facilitate online discussions between instructors and students. You will also help teach and or help in some of our live lectures. Note: if you want to become a lead instructor or TA, we recommend having previous teaching experience.
