ADMC

The AMC Learning Program (ALP) and De Mathematics Committee (DMC) present you the:

ALP x DMC Mathematics Competition

The ADMC (ALP x DMC Mathematics Competition) is an original competition brought to you by ALP (AMC Learning Program) and DMC (De Mathematics Competitions). Our mission is to provide a competition that contains both problems approachable for people who are just starting competition math and challenging for seasoned math competitors. The contest will be run on the Mathematical Advancement Committee's platform, admc.mathadvance.org; competitors will use this to access the competition rounds.

Format/Rules

There will be 4 rounds.

  • Individual Round #1:
    • There will be 10 questions to be solved in 45 minutes.
    • Each correct answer is worth 1 point. Each incorrect/blank answer is worth 0 points.
    • The difficulty will range from early AMC 12 to mid AIME.
  • Individual Round #2:
    • There will be 10 questions to be solved in 60 minutes.
    • Each correct answer is worth 1 point. Each incorrect/blank answer is worth 0 points.
    • The difficulty will range from early AMC 12 to late AIME.
  • Team Round:
    • There will be 10 questions to be solved in 60 minutes.
    • Each correct answer is worth 2 points. Each incorrect/blank answer is worth 0 points.
    • The difficulty will range from early AMC 12 to beyond late AIME.
    • You must collaborate with only your team members. Your team must agree upon a platform in which you will communicate; the competition managers will not be able to help with this. Any reported collaboration between members of different teams during this round will be investigated and will likely lead to disqualification.
  • Tiebreaker Round: TBD.
All competitors must be in 12th grade or below in the 2021-22 school year. The Tiebreaker Round will only be given to the top scoring individuals, but everyone is allowed to view the Tiebreaker Round while it is in progress since it will be held on Zoom. The Awards Ceremony will be held on Zoom, and its recording will be posted on the ALP YouTube channel on the same day.
