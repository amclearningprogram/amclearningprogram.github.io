Classes

The focal point of ALP is the live lectures. These lectures are for people who have taken the AMCs before and want to do better next year or who missed out this year and want to prepare for next year.

Students should be enthusiastic about math and wish to attend the lectures on their initiative. We educate students on learning math and pique their interest in the subject. However, we do not guarantee results, and that is not the essential focus of this program.

Logistics

This program covers the following four subjects: Algebra, Combinatorics, Geometry, and Number Theory. There will be four lectures for all of these subjects except Number Theory, which will have five lectures. We will be covering material pertinent to the AMC.

We will not be covering all of the subjects at the same time; instead, we will be splitting the subjects to be taught in two sessions. Each session lasts four weeks, and each session covers two subjects. Session one consists of Algebra and Number Theory, and Session two consists of Geometry and Combinatorics.

There will be one lecture per week for each subject, and the lectures for different subjects occur on different days. (IE. If you wanted to take both Geometry and Combinatorics, you would be able to attend both lectures within the same week.) Each subject will have its lectures occuring at the same time each week.

To attend a lecture, you must RSVP for it using the respective form found under the Schedule page (this also contains the time and the dates of the lectures), and around one day before the lecture, we will email you with the Zoom information. After each lecture, we will post the handout and a Youtube link to the recording on the Materials page.
