Classes

The focal point of ALP is the live lectures. These lectures are for people who have taken the AMCs before and want to do better next year or who missed out this year and want to prepare for next year.

Students should be enthusiastic about math and wish to attend the lectures on their initiative. We educate students on learning math and pique their interest in the subject. However, we do not guarantee results, and that is not the essential focus of this program.

Logistics

This program covers the four main subjects: Algebra, Combinatorics, Geometry, and Number Theory. There will be four lectures for all four of these subjects, except Number Theory, which has five instead. Everything we cover is essential to that subject to the AMC (and possibly AIME).

The same instructor(s) teach the lectures under any given subject, and the time of those lectures under an individual subject is the same every week.

  • Ephram Chun is teaching the Algebra lectures.
  • Evan Chang is teaching the Combinatorics lectures.
  • Chirag Samantaroy is teaching the Geometry lectures.
  • Aiden Wen and Ryan Shin are teaching the Number Theory lectures.

For any individual lecture, you have the option to RSVP to that lecture. If you want to attend multiple lectures, you need to RSVP to every one of those lectures. You can RSVP by filling out the respective google form, and a day before the lecture, you will receive the Zoom information.

After each lecture, the handout (which includes a problem set that students can work on for practice afterward) and the recording will be posted on the website and YouTube, respectively.

The Schedule page consists of the timing of the lectures and their respective RSVP forms. The Materials page consists of the handout and the YouTube link to the recording for each class.

If you want to get a brief overview of any lecture for context, you can check out the Syllabus page.
